Geographies of Trash is an ongoing public art practice by Miami artist Ahol Sniffs Glue in which discarded objects are transformed into artworks and returned to the street. Through cycling across Miami, the work becomes a practice of attention — shifting from routine to awareness, from what is overlooked to what is seen. Documented across more than 3,000 interventions, the project maps the geographies of urban neglect through creative reuse, generating a living provenance archive of found objects, GPS-logged rides, and on-chain records. Exhibited at The Wolfsonian–FIU and at the 61st International Art Exhibition, La Biennale di Venezia — recognized by six municipal governments and studied as a model for community-engaged environmental education.

The Project

Ahol Sniffs Glue is a Miami-based artist working at the intersection of street art, cycling, and environmental practice. Born in Hialeah, he has exhibited internationally and built one of the most active collector communities in contemporary street art.

Geographies of Trash is an ongoing public art practice in which discarded objects found across Miami are transformed into artworks and returned to the street. Each piece is documented, GPS-logged, and archived — generating a living provenance record of found objects, rides, and on-chain records.

Exhibited at The Wolfsonian–FIU and at the 61st International Art Exhibition, La Biennale di Venezia. Recognized by six municipal governments. Studied as a model for community-engaged environmental education.

A living archive. The pieces logged here are what we've been able to verify. Geographies of Trash is ongoing and, by nature, uncontained — there's always more trash on the streets than a database can hold. Some pieces were collected before they could be logged, never publicly claimed, or are simply gone. This is the record we can stand behind.
